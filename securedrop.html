Before You Begin — Operational Security

  • Use a public Wi-Fi network only. Never access this portal from home, work, or any network linked to your identity. Coffee shops, libraries, and airports reduce location attribution significantly.
  • Boot into Tails OS from a USB drive. Download Tails from tails.boum.org. Tails leaves no trace on the computer you use. It routes all traffic through Tor automatically. Do not use your normal operating system.
  • Strip ALL metadata from your documents before uploading. See the Metadata Scrubbing section below. EXIF data in a single photo can reveal your GPS location, device model, and the exact time it was taken.
  • Generate and save your secret codename (Step 3 below) before submitting. This is the only way journalists can respond to you. No email, no account — only your codename.
  • Never tell anyone you submitted here. Do not search for this .onion address from your regular browser. Do not discuss it via email or messaging apps. Assume all clearnet communications are monitored.

Metadata Scrubbing — Critical Step

⚠ What is EXIF Data?

Every digital file contains hidden metadata. A JPEG photo stores GPS coordinates, camera model, lens serial number, and precise timestamp. A Word document stores the author's name, company, and edit history. A PDF stores the software version and username that created it.

How to scrub metadata before uploading:
— Linux/Tails: mat2 --inplace yourfile.pdf
— Images: exiftool -all= yourphoto.jpg
— Documents: Open in LibreOffice → File → Properties → Delete Personal Data
— Universal: Convert to plain text and re-type manually if possible.
